# John the Painter:

- an old sailor sits in a Portsmouth pub, a clay pipe in his mouth and a small glass corked bottle, showing out of his pocket;
- elsewhere, elsewhen, a young man in his twenties listens to the conversation in an Oxford pub, John the Painter, aka Jack, aka other things, but we'll call him Jack, a Scotsman, a painter, dreamed of a commission in the army, went to America to join the war (American War of Independence) but soon returned; hears a tale in the Oxford pub: if you burn down the dockyard and the fleet can't operate; suggests what he can do to American representative in Paris...
- he has a gadget...; tries to set a fire in Portsmouth, fails at first attempt then succeeds, (but fire put out before too much damage)
- description put out; coincidentally, just been arrested for thieving; interrogated; divulged story to a confidant/spy; charged at Winchester, sentenced to hang from highest scaffold (a ship's mast) on the Hard; hung in chains; head is shown around the pubs;
- the Ship runs a good business renting rooms by the hour; also filled when the head is on show; the head goes off on tour, but business upstairs disrupted by a ghost; an old sailor in the corner who sells favourable winds to the sailors says he may be able to help, for a fee (free beer + pies); goes upstairs, tricks ghost into a bottle, stoppers it up, gets fee/drinks self to death, but still there in the pub, in the corner... the ghost who drove out another
- (the truth of the tale is... head was s/one else's (David Tyies, quartered on Southsea common); the irons in which John the Pianter hung in Winchester museum today)

# England Dreaming
