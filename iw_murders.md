Edinburgh Evening News - Tuesday 13 November 1877

THE MURDER AT ISLE OF WIGHT.

At the Winchester Assizes yesterday, Alfred Jones and John Brading were charged with the wilful murder of Edward James Rendell at Carisbrooke, Isle of Wight. On August 28th the body of deceased, with head fractured, was found in a brick kiln. Prisoners, who had been seen company of the deceased, were arrested, and their clothes were found stained with blood. Each was sentenced to twenty years' penal servitude.

Salisbury and Winchester Journal - Saturday 17 November 1877

THE ISLE WIGHT MURDER.

*Alfred Jones*, and *Charles Brading* were indicted for the wilful murder of Edward James Rendall, Carisbrooke, on August 28th. Mr. Collins, Q.C., and Mr. Temple Cooke prosecuted. Mr. Bullen defended Brading; and Mr. C. Mathews defended Jones.

It appeared that the deceased, native of Yeovil, was a turnip hoer, and had been in the habit for many years of going to Budbridge `[presumably, Great Budrbidges Manor, Arreton]` in order to work for Mr. Tharle. In June last commenced work and continued Mr. Tharle's employment until August 24th. On the morning of tho 28th the same month the body of the deceased was found near a stable in a dreadfully mutilated condition. The prisoners were in company with the deceased drinking on the previous night, and a stick which was proved to belong to one of them was found near the body. The three were heard quarrelling but no one appeared to have seen blow struck.

The case occupied nearly the whole of Monday and resulted in the jury convicting the prisoners of manslaughter. They were sentenced to 20 years' penal servitude.

Yorkshire Post and Leeds Intelligencer - Tuesday 27 November 1877

MURDER IN THE ISLE OF WIGHT.

A barbarous murder has been committed Elmfield, about a mile from Ryde, Isle of Wight. A young woman named Barber has for the past few weeks been living there with a labourer whose name is unknown, except that Is called "Jimmy." A neighbour yesterday afternoon, having seen the woman run out of the house bleeding, went inside and remonstrated with the man, who then put down an axe he had been handling. The neighbour left, and afterwards the body of the woman was found dreadfully mutilated. "Jimmy" is believed to have crossed Portsmouth.

Dundee Courier - Monday 03 December 1877

THE ISLE OF WIGHT MURDER.

At Newport, Isle of Wight, Petty Sessions on Saturday James Caffyn was committed for trial for the wilful murder of Maria Barber, with whom he lived, on November 20. Prisoner admitted that, being determined his victim should not deceive any more men, he deliberately took the axe and struck her several times. He did the deed with a good heart, and was willing to die for the samo.

THE ISLE OF WIGHT .MURDERER.

The execution of Caffyn, the isle of Wight murderer, is fixed for Monday, the 22th instant. He is reported to maintain the same stolid demeanour which exhibited at the trial, although he pays every attention to the prison chaplains exhortations. Marwood will be the executioner.

Western Daily Press - Tuesday 12 February 1878

THE ISLE OF WIGHT MURDER.

Winchester, Monday.—James Caffyn was executed at eight o'clock this morning for the murder of Maria Barber, at Elmfleld, Ryde, Isle of Wight. Prisoner slept well till six o'clock this morning, when he ate a hearty breakfast. He walked with a firm step to the scaffold, attended by the officials. His demeanour, that of cold, stolid indifference, did not alter from the first, but he was evidently a reserved man. Nevertheless he has paid every attention to the exhortation of the chaplain. Death was immediate.

MURDER ON THE ISLE OF WIGHT.

Newport, Isle of Wight, Friday.— This afternoon a man named Morris Holbrook gave himself up to ths Newport police, stating that he had cut a boy's throat in the barrack field at Parkhurst, and producing a knife with which he said the deed was committed. The police, on going to the spot indicated. found the body boy with two deep gashes in the throat. Ths deceased has not yet been identified.
